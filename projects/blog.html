Hexaly Blog Refresh

Redesign the company blog landing page to provide an intuitive and adapted user experience, including a new sidebar with a custom article filtering system.

Background

The company blog page aims to provide potential customers and developers with the latest news on software releases, company events, and most importantly to highlight competitive benchmarks and showcase the results of projects undertaken with clients in a multitude of industries.
This project was accomplished in my current position as a frontend software developer at Hexaly (ex-Localsolver).

Goals

When a user arrives on the blog page, the user should be able to quickly and efficiently identify blog articles that are relevant to them. The blog page should take full advantage of the space available and include direct calls to action to encourage the user to engage with the company brand.

The initial blog page layout

Starting Point

The initial blog page includes a simple, single column layout which displays the articles in order of the date they were posted. There are no tools provided to refine a search. The article cards do not make efficient use of the available space and including the beginning paragraph of the article is unnecessary given the detailed article titles.

Tools

• Figma
• Wordpress
• PHP
• HTML/CSS

Solutions

I wanted to give this page a clean and more modern look while staying within the visual identity established throughout the rest of the company website. Breaking the articles into the three main categories of ‘category’, ‘industry’, and ‘problem type’ was important to ensure the filtering system catered to all of our clients.

1: Easily filter articles by category, industry, and problem type with a clean and intuitive sidebar design. Sort articles by post date or by alphabetical order.

2: Implement a 2 column grid to display more articles at once, with more compact and straightforward cards.

3: Maximize the space in the sidebar by adding brand social media accounts. Include a call to action to encourage the user to start a free trial

Results

The refreshed blog landing page encourages the user to interact and seek out the articles that will interest them with an intuitive filtering interface. The new two-column layout displays more articles at once providing a more polished and concise look and feel.
